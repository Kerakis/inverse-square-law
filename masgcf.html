The mAs-Grid Conversion Factor Formula

A radiographic grid is a device placed between the image receptor and the object being radiographed in order to absorb scattered radiation exiting the object. Limiting the scattered radiation reaching the image receptor improves the quality of the x-ray image by increasing radiographic contrast. Grids are identified by their grid ratio, which is the height of the lead strips inside of the grid to the space between each lead strip. The table below shows the most common grid ratios and the corresponding grid conversion factors.

Grid Ratios Grid Conversion Factor (GCF)
No Grid 1
5:1 2
6:1 3
8:1 4
12:1 5
16:1 6

Solving for the GCF uses the formula:

$$ GCF_2 = \frac{mAs_2\cdot GCF_1}{mAs_1} $$

Solving for mAs uses the formula:

$$ mAs_2 = \frac{mAs_1\cdot GCF_2}{GCF_1} $$

\( mAs_1 = \) The original mAs

\( GCF_1 = \) The original grid conversion factor

\( mAs_2 = \) The new mAs

\( GCF_2 = \) The new grid conversion factor
